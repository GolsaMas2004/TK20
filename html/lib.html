Liberalism



‘Liberal’ has meant different things in different situations at different times. Today it
means a progressive, in fact conservatives have so misused and abused the L-word that
real liberals (like myself) call ourselves progressives. It better describes the position.

A liberal today basically believes that we should measure the success of our economy
by the rise in the standard of living of the working man and his family, which is about
95% of us. -Progress- is defined as a continuously rising standard of living, each
generation doing a little better than its parents. If the economy grows, if companies do
well, everyone deserves a share in the wealth created.

For more than FORTY YEARS now, the middle class has lost ground. My generation, the
Post WWII Baby Boomers, were the first generation in US history to not do as well as
their parents, but each succeeding generation since has done a little worse! We have
grown slowly poorer and poorer, while the richest 1% has doubled their wealth, and their
share of ownership, every 10 years or so. This is why we liberals and progressives whine
so much. I don’t understand why more people don’t seem to be aware of it and realize
that we can change this with our vote.

Liberalism and conservatism also represent two different economic theories.
Conservatism today (actually neo-conservatism, the Reaganomics philosophy) wants a
top down economy. The govt. helps corporations and rich investors, and then they
invest more to make more stuff so there are more and better jobs, so everyone is happy.
Liberalism believes the govt. should help people directly, and businesses would benefit
from a healthier market, people growing a little richer every year and buying more
things. So everyone would be happy. Trickle-down vs trickle-up.

The Democratic Party is not really liberal. Presidents Clinton and Obama still cared way
too much about the rich, and even during their terms wealth grew more and more
concentrated. Clinton moved the Democrats to the right to win in 1992. President
Obama bailed out the banks, reimbursing them for the mortgages that had defaulted.
But he could have given the money to the mortgage holders themselves, so they could
PAY them. The banks would have ended up with the money and people could have
saved their homes. Instead millions of Americans lost their homes! The banks did fine,
though. That’s not what a liberal would have done.
Liberals DON’T want just more and more government for some demented reason. But
there are certain things govt. has to do or they won’t get done. Education, national
infrastructure, law enforcement, stuff like that. Liberals would like to see affordable
health care with universal coverage, and that will only happen with the govt. Getting
involved. A single payer plan like almost every other developed country has. That’s
another liberal thing these days.
